# Jiayi (Johnny) Li — Personal Portfolio

Personal portfolio website for Jiayi (Johnny) Li, an undergraduate researcher at the University of Toronto studying Computer Science and Quantitative Biology. The site is designed to serve as a living resume — a single place where researchers, hiring managers, and collaborators can get a complete picture of my academic and professional background.

## What's Here

The portfolio covers the full scope of my work across research, publications, industry experience, and personal projects. The research section spans across Harvard Medical School, the University of Toronto, Sunnybrook Hospital, and Northwestern University, covering areas including kidney genetics, radiation dosimetry, Parkinson's disease protein aggregation, CMV epidemiology and more. Publications include two peer-reviewed first-author papers, a co-authored book, and more. Work experience ranges from a pharmaceutical chemical analysis internship at Pharmaron Beijing to an AI product management role at a Toronto edtech startup. Projects include a financial contagion simulator, a statistical healthcare analysis, and more all built in Python or R.

The site also includes a skills breakdown across programming languages, lab techniques, and data analysis methods; an achievements section covering hockey (OHL Priority Selection, China National Winter Games MVP), academic competitions (Canadian Chemistry Olympiad), and research recognition (AAAS Annual Meeting, CCIR Symposium to Nobel Laureate Thomas R. Cech); and a downloadable resume.
