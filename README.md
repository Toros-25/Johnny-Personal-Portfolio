# Jiayi (Johnny) Li — Personal Portfolio

Personal portfolio website for Jiayi (Johnny) Li, an undergraduate researcher at the University of Toronto studying Computer Science and Quantitative Biology. The site is meant to work as a living resume, so researchers, hiring managers, and collaborators can find my academic and professional background in one place.

## What's Here

The site pulls together my research, publications, industry experience, and personal projects. The research section covers work at Harvard Medical School, the University of Toronto, Sunnybrook Hospital, and Northwestern University, across kidney genetics, radiation dosimetry, Parkinson's disease protein aggregation, and CMV epidemiology. Publications include two peer-reviewed first-author papers and a co-authored book. Work experience runs from a pharmaceutical chemical analysis internship at Pharmaron Beijing to an AI product management role at a Toronto edtech startup, and is filterable by role type (internship vs. part-time). Projects include a financial contagion simulator, a full-stack grade-prediction web app (GradeMaster), and a statistical healthcare analysis, filterable by language, with project cards linking to GitHub, live demos, and PDF write-ups where available.

The site also includes a skills breakdown across programming languages, lab techniques, and data analysis methods; an achievements section covering hockey (OHL Priority Selection, China National Winter Games MVP), academic competitions (Canadian Chemistry Olympiad), and research recognition (AAAS Annual Meeting, CCIR Symposium to Nobel Laureate Thomas R. Cech); and a downloadable resume.
